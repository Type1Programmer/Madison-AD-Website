Interview

"Courage is what it takes to stand up and speak. Courage is also what it takes to sit down and listen." - Winston Churchill

The Interview

Getting Ready

Interview is typically a seven minute event which involves three judges asking a decathlete questions about his/her future interest, and current insights. The student must keep eye contact during the interview, and the student must answer questions with frequent elaboration. At Madison, we practice interviewing each other often, but most strenuously when we are close to state competition. We practice answering questions with elaboration and sitting with perfect posture.

The Interview

Gestures

Gestures are an important part of the interview. When first walking in, the decathlete should give a firm handshake to the judges, depending on their current hand placement and activity. The decathlete should keep hands down on their legs until he/she speaks. Madison students should keep their hands warm before the interview because points are deducted for cold hands. Include a hand gesture in your interview every minute or so to demonstrate an action.
