The History of Academic Decathlon

The team that never stops achieving.

The United States Academic Decathlon was founded in the year 1968. It all started with a man named Dr. Robert Peterson. He dreamed of creating an academic program while he was a prisoner of war in Nazi Germany. Twenty five years later, when he became a superintendent he advocated for a competiton that integrates not only greatness as he called it, but maturity and self-fulfillment. There would be three teams with three members each for a school: A, B and C. The teams would work vigorously, whether it's the A team improving social skills or C team finding new study techniques. The first national competition would be held in Bolsa Grande, California in 1982.

Madison's AD History

The rise of champions.

Madison Academic's Decathlon Team was started back in 2004. Before the leadership of Mrs. Paula Maxon and Mrs. Linda Lane, the team's coach was Mr. Don Roe. He created a team of hard-working, genius workers who never gave up. In so doing, his reign brought Madison Academic four titles in first place: 2004, 2007, 2008, and 2011. After a second place crown in 2012, Mrs. Paula Maxon was given the baton to lead the team. During her reign, we have consistently gained momentum to bring a first place trophy in our hands. Three years later, Mrs. Linda Lane joined the family. These two stellar coaches bring a unperishable hope and spark to the team that proves we are already champions, with or without a gold banner. Our team has continuously dominated the state competition, thanks to stellar coaching, 14 times in a row and counting!
